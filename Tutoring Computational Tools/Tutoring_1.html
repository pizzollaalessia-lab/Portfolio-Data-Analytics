Focus on BTP:

  • bond: obligations, such as btp in the italian scenario, through which governement finances public debt.

  • face value (fv): what the investor is receiving at MATURITY (end of the contract) fv of 100 means is standard convention

  • price (P): purchase price. if p > fv -> capital loss

  • coupons: payments based on interest, typically received every six months (however, this may change).

Ex 6: BTP and vectorization

You have a bond (BTP4% 01Ago2020) with a face value of 100 (standard convention), price \(P = 98\), and it pays coupons every six months, having t = 0 May 1st, 2019. The cash flows (including the price paid at t=0 ) can be stored in a vector cf.

Define a function npv(r) that calculates the Net Present Value.

hint: formulation will be like: \[NPV(r) = -P + \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{C_i}{(1+r)^{t_i}} + \frac{FV}{(1+r)^{t_n}}\]

Useful challenge: Instead of writing the sum explicitly ($… + … + …$), try to use vector operations inside the function. (e.g., sum(cf * …)).

  1. Use uniroot to find the Yield to Maturity (ytm; the \(r\) that makes NPV = 0).

Ex 7: root-finding with dataframes

Choosing the interval: Before calculating, estimate the sign of model(0): (Is the mean of Sepal Length positive or negative?) hint: To guarantee the existence of a root in the interval, we rely on the theorem for which: \[F(a)*f(b)<0\] hence we need to find an interval \([a, b]\) such that the function changes sign.
